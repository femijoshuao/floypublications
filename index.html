Latest Publications

The Surrender

A riveting tale of the common dilemma, temptations and challenges young believers confront in courtship and early in marriage, told in a most uncommon way. For a debutante, Princess Oni spins an incredible narrative that serves more than the didactic and moral messages normally associated with Christian literature, but also betrays uncanny literary genius and creativity in the inventive and somewhat suspenseful manner she wove the plot, giving it a most dramatic and unexpected cathartic ending.

The Quintessential Bureaucrat

This book tracks the rise of the 11th indigenous Registrar of the prestigious Indigenous University of Ibadan, Mr.Olujimi Iretola Olukoya’s meteoric rise from a young career officer to the pinnacle of higher education administration in Nigeria’s premier university.

Guide to Peaceful Living

Guide to Peaceful Living is a life coach book offering advice on how to achieve a happy, contented and fulsome life, drawing from the author’s background and experiences. The book points the reader to the fact that he is responsible for the choices he makes and their consequences . It emphasises the need for man to consciously adopt the right approach in dealing with and winning in various situations he may confront in life… It also shares salient tips on strategies and principles for a stress-free life and counsels the reader on need for regular appraisal and adjustment of his positive and negative traits relative to opportunities and threats to ensure outcomes that further him in every way!

A Reporter And His Beat

This book provides a comprehensive insight of a reporter on the beat in Africa’s greatest country, Nigeria. Apart from surveying the trends and dynamics of journalism training and practice, media ecologies, ethos and traditions over time, it also reflects the sociology of the Nigerian media. In it, Yinka Fabowale, one of Nigeria’s most accomplished and award- winning journalists, vividly paints the picture of the diverse experiences and challenges the African journalist faces as he rises from the level of a rookie to the zenith of his career.

Federal College of Education (Special) Oyo

The publication chronicles the growth of the first institution, set up in 1977 to train manpower for special education needs of persons with physical, psychological, social and environmental impediments in West Africa. It details the phenomenal progress the college recorded in terms of infrastructural and capacity development as well as service delivery to the Nigerian public in its 40 years of existence under various leadership.
It was published to mark the 40th anniversary of the college that advanced from a mere teacher training school into a degree -awarding college affiliated to the University of Ibadan.
